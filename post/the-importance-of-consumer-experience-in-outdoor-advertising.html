The Importance of Consumer Experience in Outdoor Advertising

Outdoor advertising (OOH) is one of the oldest and most effective forms of marketing, as it allows for broad and diverse audience reach in different public spaces such as public roads, subway stations, shopping centers, and large commercial hubs. However, for this advertising to be effective, it is necessary to consider the consumer's experience as a crucial factor.

Consumer Experience in Outdoor Advertising

The consumer's experience is the set of emotions, impressions, and sensations that a consumer has when interacting with a product, service, or brand. In out-of-home advertising, this experience is even more relevant, as consumers are on the move and have only a few seconds to remember the advertisement. Therefore, in order for the advertising to be effective, it is necessary to create attractive and memorable messages that engage and motivate consumers.

Why is consumer experience so important in out-of-home advertising?

The consumer experience is crucial because it allows for creating an emotional connection between the brand and the consumer. When a consumer interacts with out-of-home advertising, they are largely driven by their emotions and perception. Therefore, it's necessary to create an experience that's attractive, easy to understand, and memorable, so that the consumer never forgets the brand.

To create an attractive and effective consumer experience, it's important to consider some factors, such as the location of the advertisement, how the message is presented, and the interaction between the consumer and the brand. Additionally, it's also crucial to provide the consumer with a pleasant and comfortable environment, so that they can feel safe and well-being, which increases the chance that they will remember the brand and the message.

Examples of Consumer Experience in Out-of-Home Advertising

A classic example of consumer experience in out-of-home advertising is the use of projection screens in major shopping centers. These screens allow for an immersive and interactive experience, where consumers can interact with the advertisement and remember the brand. Another example is the use of outdoor advertising with attractive and easily memorable messages to attract the attention of moving consumers.

But did they forget? What negative consequences can the consumer experience be?

Yes, it's true. A negative customer experience can have negative consequences for the brand, such as loss of trust and disloyalty, which may lead to a loss of customers and revenue. Therefore, it is essential to create a consumer experience that is appealing and easy to remember, allowing for an emotional connection between the brand and the consumer.

In conclusion, the consumer experience is key in out-of-home advertising. It's necessary to create an attractive and easy-to-remember experience that allows for emotional connection between the brand and the consumer. Therefore, it's fundamental to consider the consumer experience as a crucial factor in out-of-home advertising, to create a memorable and effective experience.
