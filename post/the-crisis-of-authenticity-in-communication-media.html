The Crisis of Authenticity in Communication Media

The Crisis of Authenticity in Communication Media

Authenticity is a fundamental value in any human relationship. In particular, in the media, authenticity is essential for people to trust information vehicles. However, in recent times, the crisis of authenticity in the media has been a recurring problem.

The crisis of authenticity in the media is the result of several causes, including the quest for audience and high competition among the media. This has led to a lack of information verification and the dissemination of false news. For example, the news about Donald Trump having kicked out a leg high in the 2016 presidential debate was erroneously published by more than 45 media sources.

Why authenticity is so important in means of communication?

Authenticity is essential because it provides credibility to information sources. When people trust authentic means of communication, they can make informed decisions. Additionally, authenticity helps pre-judge people about the information they receive, which helps prevent the spread of fake news.

However, the crisis of authenticity in the media also has negative social effects. The dissemination of fake news can lead to a more polarized and divided population. Additionally, the lack of authenticity can erode public trust in governments and institutions, which can have grave consequences for social stability.

Resolution

Therefore, to solve the crisis of authenticity in the media, it is essential that information vehicles believe in sources and verify information before publishing them. Moreover, it is important for people to develop their critical skills to evaluate information and distinguish between truth and falsehood.

How can I protect myself from fake news and disinformation?

You can protect yourself from fake news and misinformation by verifying your sources before sharing information. Additionally, it's important to avoid sharing news without verifying if they have reliable sources. You can also use tools to verify information, such as fact-checking, to ensure that the information you are receiving is accurate and authentic.
