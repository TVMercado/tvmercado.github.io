Ethics of Advertising and Perceived Value

Perceived Value and Advertising: Ethical Implications

Advertising is a fundamental element in marketing and the corporate sector. It allows companies to promote their products and services, attracting customers and generating revenue. However, over the years, advertising has evolved significantly, becoming more sophisticated and personalized. However, this evolution also brought new ethical questions about how companies use consumer information for promotional purposes.

What is this?

The perceived value, also known as the consumer-perceived value, refers to the feeling that consumers have about the value they get in relation to the cost of a product or service. This concept is fundamental in marketing, as it allows companies to better understand their customers and optimize their marketing strategies.

What is the ethical impact of advertising and perceived value on consumers?

There are various ethical concerns related to advertising and perceived value. For example, the way companies collect and use consumers' data for advertising purposes can violate those individuals' privacy. Additionally, advertising can influence consumers' perception of products and services, making them vulnerable to manipulation.

One of the main ethical concerns is the violation of privacy. Companies obtain personal data from consumers through their online platforms, social media, and other sources. These data are used to better understand consumer habits and preferences, which helps companies create more effective advertising. However, this practice can violate consumers' privacy and cause harm to their online security.

Impacts on Consumers

Advertising and perceived value can have negative impacts on consumers. Exposure to advertisements may influence consumer perception of products and services, making them vulnerable to manipulation. Furthermore, violation of privacy can cause stress and loss of trust in businesses.

What Can Companies Do?

Companies can take measures to reduce their ethical impacts in advertising and perceived value. In the first place, they must ensure that consumer data is collected and used transparently and with user consent. Additionally, companies can implement rigorous privacy policies and provide clear information on how they use consumer data.

Why Ethics of Advertising is Important?

Advertising ethics is important because it seeks to ensure that companies respect consumer rights and privacy, making advertising more ethical and transparent. When companies respect consumers, they can build trust and increase customer satisfaction.

In summary, perceived value and advertising can have significant ethical impacts on consumers. It is important that companies are aware of these impacts and take measures to reduce them. Advertising ethics are fundamental to ensuring that companies respect consumer rights and privacy, making advertising more ethical and transparent.
